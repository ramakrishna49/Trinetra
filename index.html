FAQ

What exactly do we check during inspection?

Explore detailed questions under each category – from dimensions to brand checks.

We compare room sizes, wall-to-wall distances, door heights and window dimensions with the approved floor plan. This helps identify any major deviations that can impact usage or furniture placement.

We perform sample measurements of key rooms to check if the usable space broadly matches what is mentioned in the plan. For exact legal verification, you should also consult your builder and legal advisor.

We assess workmanship, finishing quality, alignment, sharp edges, paint consistency, cleanliness of joints, grout lines, and hardware installation. The idea is to ensure your home feels complete and ready to move-in.

Yes. Observations are tagged as Minor / Major / Critical so that you and your builder can prioritise rectification based on severity and impact.

We inspect for hollow tiles using tapping methods, check for lippage (uneven tile levels), cracked or chipped tiles, improper grout filling and tile alignment patterns across the rooms.

Yes, we check tiles in toilets, balconies and utility areas for cracks, slope towards the drain, water stagnation and proper finishing around floor traps.

We check water pressure, flow at taps, leakages under basins, WC fittings, flush performance, drain outlets, geyser points and basic plumbing safety to ensure no visible leakage or backflow.

Where hot water supply is available, we run tests on those lines as well. We also check basic alignment and operation of taps, diverters, mixers and shower fittings.

We observe ceilings for cracks, undulations, paint patches, damp marks, water seepage signs near beams, duct areas, and around ceiling corners or light points.

Yes, where false ceilings are installed, we visually check for gaps, sagging, alignment and visible moisture or access issues around light and AC cut-outs.

We check for cracks, hollow sounds, damp patches, poor putty finish, paint shade variation, and alignment issues especially near columns, beams and corners.

Yes, wherever possible, observations are supported with photographs and clear descriptions so that the builder’s team can easily locate and fix them.

We inspect DB, MCB arrangement, basic earthing checks (where feasible), switchboard operation, point mapping, socket polarity (basic tester) and visible wiring safety around the home.

Yes, we test accessible light points, fans and sockets for basic functionality. If a point is not live due to builder’s supply status, it will be mentioned in the report.

We check shutter operation, alignment, gap between frame and wall, lock and latch functioning, stopper performance, glass installation and basic sealing against dust/water ingress.

Yes, we verify track smoothness, locking, mesh frame stability and visible sealing for balcony sliders and mesh shutters wherever installed.

We check overall floor level, slope where required (to drains), loose or hollow sounds, chipped corners, expansion joints and overall neatness of finishing.

We make basic assessments of slipperiness, especially in toilets and balconies, and highlight surfaces that feel too smooth or risky when wet.

We look at ventilation, natural light, cross-breeze potential, placement of AC points, TV and power sockets to ensure the living area is practical and comfortable to use.

Yes, wherever practical, we highlight layout-related points such as awkward pillar positions, limited space around doors, or odd niches that may affect your furniture planning.

We use measuring tapes and laser tools to check key room sizes and compare them with plan drawings. While we do practical checks, legal certification of carpet area should be taken from your builder and legal advisor.

We do not issue legal carpet area certificates. Our checks are for practical confirmation and to flag any obvious mismatches that may require discussion with the builder.

We use visual inspection and, where required, moisture meters to detect dampness behind paint, around beams, in corners of toilets, balconies and external walls.

We highlight damp areas, patterns and likely sources, but some complex seepage issues may require further specialised tests or invasive checks by the builder’s maintenance team.

We verify whether installed materials (tiles, sanitary ware, CP fittings, switches, etc.) broadly match what was promised in the agreement or brochure, based on visible branding and specifications.

No destructive testing is done. Our focus is on visual quality, correct installation and brand/model verification wherever details are visible.

We cross-check the visible brand names on tiles, sanitary ware, CP fittings, switches and wiring (where possible) with the brands mentioned in your agreement or specification sheet.

Any visible mismatch is recorded in the report so you can take it up with the builder. Final commercial or legal discussions about brand changes should be handled by you and your legal advisor.
